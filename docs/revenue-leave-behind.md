# DNaI — Revenue Model Summary (Leave-Behind)

Cloud Control LLC · Project DNaI · July 2026

> Internal projections as of July 1, 2026. Figures are forward-looking targets, not realized results.

## What it is

DNaI turns each verified human genome into a tokenized, ownable digital asset. Buyers (pharma, research, AI) pay for consented, ZK-verified access; genome holders keep the majority of every transaction. DNaI earns a 20% protocol fee on all activity.

## Business trajectory

The plan scales across four phases from $0 to a $9M ARR run rate: pilots in Phase II ($270K ARR), mainnet access fees and subscriptions in Phase III ($3.6M ARR), and AI licensing plus marketplace volume in Phase IV ($9M ARR). The conservative bottom-up H2 2026 forecast exits December at a $1.93M ARR run rate with 10,000 genome holders and six paying enterprise customers.

## How it makes money

Seven streams anchor on the 20% protocol fee, complemented by enterprise API subscriptions ($50K–$250K/yr), sequencing-partner licensing, AI training-data licenses ($500–$5,000), compliance certifications ($25K–$100K/yr), DAO treasury yield, and a 1% marketplace listing fee. Revenue is intentionally diversified — the protocol fee is only about a third of steady-state MRR, so subscription and compliance revenue provide a floor.

## Why the economics work

Unit economics are strong on both sides of the marketplace: genome holders show a 24:1 LTV:CAC with a two-month payback, while pharma partners show 88:1 with payback in about three weeks. Blended gross margin sits near 89%, in line with best-in-class data platforms. Enterprise net revenue retention is projected at 120%.

## Pricing rationale

Pricing is value-based, not cost-plus: a pharma buyer pays under 3% of the economic value a genomic cohort unlocks, leaving most of the value with the buyer and making procurement easy to justify. Each standard transaction splits 73% to the holder, 20% to the protocol, and 7% to the DAO treasury.

## Resilience

Stress-testing shows durability: a 30%→10% collapse in access rate cuts ARR only 22%, a halved protocol fee only 17%, and a 30% price drop only 14%. Even all three adverse scenarios at once still yield ~$1.65M ARR.

## The raise

DNaI targets an $8M–$12M Series A at an $18M–$28M post-money valuation, supported by ~$2M–$3M ARR, ~89% margins, 120% NRR, and a strategic pharma anchor investor.
